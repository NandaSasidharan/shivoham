How did I become a Hatha Yoga teacher?

I was a playful, fighting and at occations shy as a child. Weaker financial situation at home and a reading habit I picked up (thanks to my dad, and a next-door library) when I was around 9, helped me become studious. I received a PhD in Electrical engineering from a US university and worked as a research fellow at a European research lab. But as a scientist, one's first most quest is to understand what life is.


About PhD Sadhguru once joked that it is Permanent head Damage. Maybe, he is right :) I learned (or experienced) more about life during my training at Isha yoga center than during my entire education.
But if I have not walked that way, I would not be here today.

Once I heard a Vedic math prose. It was a combination of scientific knowledge, mathematical precision, poetry, linguistics, and devotion in a very beautiful way. I knew whoever created it was incredible.


(Sanskrit)

gopi bhagya madhuvrata srngiso dadhi sandhiga |
khala jivita khatava gala hala rasandara ||

(English)

Oh Krishna, the fortune of the Gopis,
The distroyer of the demon Madhu,
The protector of cattle,
The one who ventured the ocean depths,
destroyer of evildoers,
one with plough on the shoulder,
and the bearer of nectar, may (you) protect (us)

The original Sanskrit prose encode the value Pi upto 31 decimal places.
[How?]

I met Sadhguru online. For the first time someone seemed to make total sense. The absolute clarity he demonstrated was first of its kind for me. I simply cannot avoid an opportunity to learn from him. But I did not trust me to be a quick and committed learner in this new domain. So I took a break from my research job, and took up intense Hatha yoga teacher training at Isha yoga center, Coimbatore, India lasting 21 weeks.


It is where I understood the difference between experiencing life and reading about it in a book. It is where I experienced the different dimensions of mind. It is where I understood what it meant to be loving. It is where I understood what it meant to be living.


Today I am here sharing what he offers while I walk my way.
