Upa-Yoga

Upa-Yoga is a simple yet powerful system of exercise that activates the joints, muscles, and energy system. Based on a sophisticated understanding of the body's mechanics, Upa Yoga dispels inertia in the body's energy and brings ease to the whole system.
Upa Yoga essentially means "sub-yoga" or "pre-yoga". Because of its many immediate and evident benefits, the word Upa Yoga in Indian languages is commonly used to denote "usefulness".
Within the human system, the energy flows along 72,000 pathways called nadis. At the joints, the nadis meet and form nodes, making the joints storehouses of energy. Upa Yoga activates this energy and also lubricates the joints, creating an instant sense of alertness and liveliness. Article: Yoga for Children

Practice requirements:

  • Suitable for ages 7+
  • Moderate-level intensity
  • No prior experience of yoga necessary

Benefits of Upa-Yoga:

  • Relieves physical stress and tiredness
  • Strengthens the joints and muscles
  • Rejuvenates the body after periods of inactivity
  • Negates the effects of jetlag and long travel

Surya Shakti

Surya means "Sun" and Shakti means "Energy". Surya Shakti is a complete process, which is largely physical in nature. If you do 108 cycles of Surya Shakti, it will make you fit and you will not need any other form of exercise. Surya Shakti builds the physical body-it makes the sinews and ligaments of your body strong. In Yoga, we give importance to sinews that holds the skeletal system and whole body together. When we do any yogic practice, which is physical in nature, the focus is mainly to strengthen those, not to pump up your muscles. Strengthening the sinews of the body is what will endure for long time and keep you well. Surya Shakti does this in a tremendous way. Surya Shakti is a dynamic form of the Sun Salutation which is an ancient yogic practice with a powerful sequence of 18 postures.

Practice requirements:

  • Suitable for ages 7+
  • Moderate-level intensity
  • No prior experience of yoga necessary

Benefits of Surya Shakti:

  • Brings about physical fitness and overall wellbeing
  • Makes the sinews and the ligaments of the body strong
  • Increases mental alertness and focus
  • Creates a basis to move into higher states of energy
  • Increases physical strength & stamina
  • Realigns the musculoskeletal system
  • Increases energy levels

Angamardana

Angamardana is a system rooted in yoga that offers everyone the opportunity to invigorate the body and reach peak physical and mental health. "Angamardana" means gaining complete mastery over the limbs, organs and other parts of the body. It strengthens the spine, builds physical strength, fitness and tenacity, taking years off the body. Designed by Sadhguru, Isha Angamardana needs no fitness equipment. It involves only the body and floor exercises that can be practiced anywhere, even during travel. More.

Bhuta Shuddhi

The system of yoga called Bhuta Shuddhi, meaning purification of the elements, is the fundamental process that underlies every other yogic practice. Simple yet powerful Bhuta Shuddhi processes are offered as a part of Isha Hatha Yoga programs, allowing one to cleanse the body and prepare for more powerful sadhanas.
The basis for all creation, including the physical body, is the group of five elements: earth, water, wind, fire, and space. The wellbeing of the body and mind can be established by purifying these five elements within the human system. This process also shapes the body into a stepping stone towards one's ultimate wellbeing. Article: Bhuta Shuddhi - A Comprehensive Guide on Its Significance, Benefits and Practices

Mantra Yoga

There is a beautiful story in the yogic lore that describes how when Adiyogi utters three AUMs, a whole new creation comes into being. The story is a dialectical expression of the possibility of the sound AUM. Known as the universal or basic sound in the existence, AUM is a combination of the three primordial syllables - "Aaa", "Ooo", and "Maa". AUM chanting is a powerful practice that aligns the three fundamental aspects of the system - body, mind and energy - leading to a more profound experience of life. It helps stabilize the body and mind, thus eliminating psychological disturbances and alleviating chronic ailments. AUM chanting creates a powerful basis for further inner exploration.

Benefits of Mantra Yoga:

  • Helps stabilize the body and mind
  • Helps come out of psychological disturbances
  • Relief from excessive fear, nightmares, unstable mind
  • Remedies attention disorders
  • Helps bring resolve in life
